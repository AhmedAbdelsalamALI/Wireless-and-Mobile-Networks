177. Absorption in wireless communication means the signal passes through obstacles without losing energy.

180. Frequency Modulation (FM) changes the amplitude of the carrier wave.

181. What is Phase Modulation (PM)?

182. Digital modulation techniques convert analog signals into digital form.

183. QPSK (Quadrature Phase Shift Keying) is a type of digital modulation.

184. What is the primary advantage of digital modulation over analog modulation?

185. OFDM (Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing) is a modulation technique used in Wi-Fi and LTE.

186. Spread Spectrum techniques are used to make signals more susceptible to interference.

187. What is the primary purpose of coding in wireless communication?

188. Forward Error Correction (FEC) adds redundant information to data for error correction.

189. Cyclic Redundancy Check (CRC) is used for error correction.

190. What is the main purpose of MIMO (Multiple-Input Multiple-Output) technology?

191. MIMO systems use only one antenna for transmission and reception.

192. Spatial multiplexing in MIMO allows transmitting multiple independent data streams simultaneously.

193. What is the primary purpose of beamforming in wireless communication?

194. Beamforming improves signal strength and reduces interference.

195. Beamforming is only used in 5G networks.

196. What is a major advantage of cognitive radio?

197. Cognitive radio can sense its environment and adapt its transmission parameters.

198. Spectrum holes are unused portions of licensed spectrum.

199. What is the main challenge in implementing cognitive radio?

200. Cognitive radio aims to improve spectrum efficiency.

201. Secondary users in cognitive radio have priority access to spectrum.

202. What is the primary purpose of a mobile ad-hoc network (MANET)?

203. MANETs have a centralized control point.

204. Nodes in a MANET can act as routers to forward data.

205. What is a major challenge in MANETs?

206. MANETs are suitable for disaster relief operations.

207. Security is not a major concern in MANETs.

208. What is the primary characteristic of a vehicular ad-hoc network (VANET)?

209. VANETs are a type of MANET.

210. VANETs are used for improving road safety and traffic efficiency.

211. What is a major challenge in VANETs?

212. Roadside Units (RSUs) in VANETs are mobile devices.

213. VANETs can facilitate communication for autonomous vehicles.

214. What is the primary characteristic of a wireless mesh network (WMN)?

215. WMNs are self-configuring and self-healing.

216. Each node in a WMN acts as a router.

217. WMNs are typically used for providing internet access in rural areas.

218. What is a major advantage of WMNs?

219. WMNs are less resilient to node failures than traditional Wi-Fi networks.

220. WMNs are primarily used for short-range personal area networks.

221. What is the primary characteristic of a wireless local area network (WLAN)?

222. WLANs typically use Wi-Fi technology.

223. An Access Point (AP) is a central device in a WLAN.

224. WLANs are suitable for connecting devices across different cities.

225. What is the primary advantage of WLANs over wired LANs?

226. WLANs typically offer higher data rates than wired LANs.

227. WLANs are commonly used in homes, offices, and public hotspots.

228. What is the primary characteristic of a wireless personal area network (WPAN)?

229. Bluetooth is a common technology used for WPANs.

230. WPANs are designed for high data rate applications like video streaming.

231. What is the typical range of a WPAN?

232. WPANs are ideal for connecting devices like headphones, keyboards, and mice.

233. WPANs primarily use licensed spectrum.

239. WMANs are primarily used for connecting devices within a single building.

243. What is the typical range of a WWAN?
